    1. Instead of appearing for the exam in May'23 and joining the campus in Aug'23, Can I instead seek an admission starting from Sept'23 for the PhD Program?

      [Click to Expand] Explanation: Since, it is being difficult to manage my memory for both UPSC-CSE (Prelims) & ISI-JRF exams, being held in the same month (May'23), taking note of the exception that exists on ISI's - Economics Planning Unit webpage attached below.
      Can I seek an admission by taking the examination whichever the admission board requires me, to start from Sept'23 - written/interview/both etc. ?


      By doing this, I can provide my maximum attention towards securing a top-rank in UPSC-CSE at first and finish off the written exams (UPSC - CSE (Mains)) by Sept'23. This would also provide me more than enough time (since I have started picking up Economics only from Oct'22) to concretely establish my foundations in Micro Theory - Choice, Equilibrium & Welfare, Game Theory, Mechanism Design, Auctions & Network Theory etc., and fill my knowledge gaps in Econometrics & Macro Theory (emphasising on parts involving micro elements) a lot more beyond the demand levels of the ISI-JRF Exam.
      Above all, I get to have enough time to do an in-depth analysis on some recent papers and understand the trends and directions of research more concretely. By getting done away with UPSC-CSE exam (**securing a top-rank**), I can whole-heartedly dedicate myself to research without having to worry about career planning and family at home much.
